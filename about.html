Founding

It started with one door that nobody else would build right.

In 1987, Robert Hollis was a finish carpenter in Brooklyn, NY, frustrated with the millwork options available to his clients. Pre-hung, hollow-core, veneered — none of it met his standard.

So he built a door himself. Solid red oak, mortise-and-tenon joinery, hand-fit and hand-finished. His client called three architect friends. Those architects called five more. Within a year, Robert had a crew of four and a waiting list.

Today, Solid Doors & Moldings operates a 40,000 square foot facility in Brooklyn, New York — with 60 craftspeople, three dedicated CNC cells, and the same rule Robert started with: if it isn't solid, we don't sell it.

History

The milestones that shaped us.

Every significant decision we've made has been about deepening our craft — not broadening our margins.

1987
Founded in a Brooklyn garage
Robert Hollis builds his first custom solid oak door in his Brooklyn garage. Word spreads through the New York architecture community.
1993
First commercial facility — 6,000 sq ft
Moved into our first proper mill shop in Bushwick, Brooklyn with 12 craftspeople.
1999
Expanded into architectural moldings
Client demand led us to add crown, base, casing, and panel molding to our product line.
2006
Fire-rated door certification
Achieved UL listing for fire-rated doors — opening the commercial and hospitality market.
2014
Current 40,000 sq ft facility opens
Moved into our purpose-built East New York facility with advanced CNC and finishing lines.
2026
37 years, 60,000+ doors delivered
Still family-owned. Still solid wood. Still the same standard Robert set on day one.

What We Believe

Our Values

01
Solid, Always

We use solid hardwood in every product we make. No veneers, no MDF cores, no shortcuts. The material is half the product — it deserves to be honest.

02
Craft Over Speed

We don't rush. Every door goes through 14 quality checkpoints. Every molding profile is checked for accuracy and consistency before it leaves the facility.

03
Lifetime Accountability

We warranty our structural work for life. If something fails due to our workmanship, we fix it — no questions, no expiration dates.

04
Transparent Process

No surprises on pricing, lead times, or specifications. You get shop drawings, progress updates, and a direct line to your project manager.

05
Sustainability

All our hardwoods are FSC-certified. Our finishing systems are low-VOC. We run a zero-landfill shop — all sawdust and offcuts are repurposed or composted.

06
American Made

Every door, every molding, every piece of hardware we sell is made or sourced in the United States. We believe in the craftspeople who work here.
